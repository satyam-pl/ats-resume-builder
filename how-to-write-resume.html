How to Write a Professional Resume in India (2026 Guide)

Writing a resume that actually gets you interviews is harder than it looks. Most Indian job seekers send the same generic resume to 50 companies and wonder why they hear nothing back. This guide will show you exactly how to write a resume that stands out — and passes ATS filters.

1. Choose the right resume format

In India, the most commonly accepted and recruiter-preferred format is the reverse chronological format — your most recent job or education comes first. This is also the safest for ATS systems.

  • Reverse chronological — Best for most candidates with work experience
  • Functional — Good if you are changing careers or have gaps
  • Combination — Mixes both; good for senior professionals

For freshers and candidates with less than 3 years experience, stick to reverse chronological. It's what 90% of Indian recruiters prefer.

2. Contact information section

Always include these at the very top of your resume:

  • Full name (large, prominent)
  • Phone number (with +91 country code)
  • Professional email (avoid cute email addresses — use your name)
  • City and state (full address not needed)
  • LinkedIn profile URL
  • GitHub / Portfolio (if relevant for IT/engineering roles)

3. Write a strong professional summary

This is 2–3 lines at the top that tells the recruiter who you are and what value you bring. This is the most important section — recruiters spend only 6 seconds on a resume at first glance.

Good example:

"Data Engineer with 3 years of experience building scalable ETL pipelines using PySpark and Azure. Skilled in reducing data processing time and improving data quality. Looking to contribute to high-impact data teams."

4. Work experience — the most important section

List your jobs in reverse order (latest first). For each job, include:

  • Job title
  • Company name
  • Dates (Month Year – Month Year)
  • 3–5 bullet points describing your work

The key to a great experience section is writing achievement-oriented bullets, not just job duties.

Bad (duty-based):

• Responsible for writing Python scripts

Good (achievement-based):

• Automated data validation using Python, reducing manual QA effort by 60%

5. Education section

Include your degree, university name, year of passing, and CGPA or percentage. For freshers, this section comes before work experience.

6. Skills section

List technical and soft skills relevant to the job. Keep it clean — use comma-separated keywords or small tags. ATS systems scan this section heavily, so include keywords from the job description.

7. Certifications

If you have relevant certifications — especially cloud certifications like AZ-900, AWS Cloud Practitioner, Google Data Analytics — include them. They significantly improve your chances in IT roles.

Common resume mistakes to avoid

  • Using a photo on your resume (not needed in India for most companies)
  • Including date of birth, religion, marital status (not relevant and outdated)
  • Writing "References available on request" — just remove it
  • Using tables, columns, or fancy designs that confuse ATS
  • Typos and grammatical errors
  • More than 2 pages for under 5 years of experience
