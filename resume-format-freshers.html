Best Resume Format for Freshers India 2026

You just graduated or are about to. You have no full-time work experience. And everyone says "experience required." So how do you write a resume that actually gets you interviews? This guide is for you.

The fresher resume structure

Unlike experienced candidates who put work experience first, freshers should arrange their resume like this:

1
Contact Information
Name, phone, email, LinkedIn, city
2
Professional Summary / Objective
2–3 lines — what you're looking for and what you bring
3
Education
Put this first — it's your strongest section as a fresher
4
Skills
Technical + soft skills, tools, languages
5
Internships / Projects
Very important — this replaces work experience
6
Certifications
Online courses, Coursera, Microsoft Learn, etc.
7
Extracurriculars / Achievements (optional)
Only if genuinely impressive

How to write the education section

This is your strongest section. Include:

  • Degree name (B.Tech, BCA, B.Sc, MBA etc.)
  • University / College name
  • Year of passing
  • CGPA or percentage — include only if 7.0+ CGPA or 65%+
  • Relevant coursework (optional): Data Structures, DBMS, Machine Learning

If your CGPA is below 7, you can skip it entirely. Never lie about your grades — it gets checked.

The internship / project section — your secret weapon

Most freshers leave this section weak. Don't. This is what separates you from other applicants. Even college projects count!

How to write a project bullet:

  • What did you build?
  • What technologies did you use?
  • What was the outcome or result?

"Built a student attendance management system using Python and MySQL. Reduced manual entry time by 70%. Deployed on a local college server for 200+ students."

Skills section for freshers

Be specific. "Good communication skills" means nothing. List concrete things:

  • Programming: Python, Java, C++
  • Databases: MySQL, MongoDB
  • Tools: Git, VS Code, Postman
  • Cloud: Azure basics, AWS fundamentals
  • Other: MS Excel, Power BI, Tableau

Certifications that boost your fresher resume

These free/cheap certifications are highly valued by Indian recruiters:

  • Microsoft AZ-900 (Azure Fundamentals) — free to learn on Microsoft Learn
  • Google Data Analytics Certificate (Coursera)
  • AWS Cloud Practitioner
  • HackerRank certifications (Python, SQL, Java)
  • NPTEL courses from IITs

Fresher resume mistakes to avoid

  • Writing "Objective: To get a good job in a reputed company" — this is useless
  • Putting your 10th and 12th marks unless specifically asked
  • Listing every subject you studied
  • Adding a photo (most companies do not want this)
  • Making the resume more than 1 page — keep it to 1 page as a fresher
  • Fancy colored templates that fail ATS

Length — 1 page only

As a fresher, your resume should be exactly 1 page. Recruiters at large companies like TCS, Wipro, Infosys process hundreds of resumes. A 2-page fresher resume signals poor judgment.
