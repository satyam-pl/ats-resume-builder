10 ATS Resume Tips to Get Shortlisted Faster

Over 75% of resumes are rejected by ATS (Applicant Tracking Systems) before a human ever sees them. Companies like TCS, Infosys, Accenture, and most startups use ATS to filter thousands of applications. Here's exactly how to beat it.

What is ATS?

ATS stands for Applicant Tracking System. It's software that automatically scans your resume for keywords, format, and structure before a recruiter reads it. If your resume doesn't match, it gets rejected automatically — no human ever sees it.

Estimated 75–90% of large Indian companies now use some form of ATS. Even if you're perfectly qualified, a bad resume format can get you filtered out.

10 tips to make your resume ATS-friendly

1

Use a single-column layout

Two-column resumes confuse ATS parsers. The system reads left to right, top to bottom. Stick to one clean column. Our builder uses this format automatically.

2

Use standard section headings

Name your sections exactly: "Work Experience", "Education", "Skills", "Certifications". Creative names like "My Journey" or "What I've Done" confuse ATS systems.

3

Match keywords from the job description

Read the job posting carefully. If it says "PySpark", make sure your resume says "PySpark" — not just "big data tools". ATS matches exact keywords.

4

Avoid tables, columns, and text boxes

Tables and text boxes are often invisible to ATS. All your content should be in regular paragraph or bullet form.

5

No images, icons, or graphics

Profile photos, logos, and design elements cannot be read by ATS. A fancy designed resume will often score zero.

6

Use standard fonts

Stick to fonts like Calibri, Arial, Times New Roman, or Georgia. Fancy fonts may not render correctly when the ATS parses the document.

7

Submit as PDF (usually)

PDF preserves formatting. Most modern ATS systems read PDF well. Check the job posting — if it specifically asks for .docx, use that.

8

Spell out abbreviations once

Write "Natural Language Processing (NLP)" the first time, then use "NLP" after. ATS may not recognize abbreviations alone.

9

Include a skills section with exact tool names

List each skill individually: Python, SQL, Azure, PySpark, Tableau, Power BI. Don't write "proficient in various data tools".

10

Keep file name professional

Name your resume file: FirstName-LastName-Resume.pdf. Not "resume_final_v3_new.pdf".

What ATS does NOT care about

  • Colors and design — it's invisible to ATS
  • How impressive your photo looks
  • Custom icons next to section titles
  • How creative your layout is

This is why a clean, simple resume beats a fancy designed one every time when it comes to getting past ATS.
